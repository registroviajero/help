# What is RegistroViajero

RegistroViajero is an online platform that simplifies **Royal Decree 933/2021** compliance for tourist accommodations in Spain. It collects guest data, verifies identity documents, and automatically submits the mandatory reports to the Ministry of the Interior.

## How it works

### 1. Register your accommodations

Add each of your accommodations with its address, type (apartment, hotel, rural house, etc.), and establishment code issued by the Ministry. You can manage multiple accommodations from a single agency account.

### 2. Create reservations

When you receive a booking, create it by setting the check-in and check-out dates. You can also **import reservations automatically** from platforms like Booking.com, Airbnb, or VRBO by connecting their [iCal calendar](/en/guide/ical).

### 3. Send the check-in link

Each guest receives a [unique link](/en/guide/check-in). No sign-up, no installation — they open the link on their phone or browser and fill in their details.

### 4. Review and validate

Once every guest has completed their data, review it and mark the reservation as validated.

### 5. Submit to the Ministry

With one click, RegistroViajero generates the XML documents, compresses them, and [submits them to SES.HOSPEDAJES](/en/guide/submit) at the Ministry of the Interior.

## Requirements to get started

1. **Agency details:** trading name and CIF/NIF
2. **Accommodation details:** address, type, establishment code
3. **SES.HOSPEDAJES credentials:** username, password, and lessor code issued by the Ministry
4. **Web browser** — Chrome, Safari, Firefox, or Edge (current versions)

::: tip
If you don't have Ministry credentials yet, you can still use RegistroViajero to manage reservations and collect guest data. Ministry submission can be enabled later.
:::

## Current limitations

### Regions with their own police services

RegistroViajero currently submits data to the **SES.HOSPEDAJES platform** at the Ministry of the Interior, which covers most Spanish regions.

**Not yet supported:**

- **Catalonia** (Mossos d'Esquadra) — Barcelona, Girona, Lleida, Tarragona
- **Basque Country** (Ertzaintza) — Álava, Gipuzkoa, Biscay

These regions use their own systems. Integration is planned for future releases.
